# Competitive Notes

## Rollio

Reference:

- `Rollio: Light Meter & Film Log`
- App Store listing: <https://apps.apple.com/us/app/rollio-light-meter-film-log/id6744120369>

Observed positioning:

- `Light Meter & Film Log`
- `Track analog rolls & exposure`
- free app with optional in-app purchases
- privacy posture presented as `Data Not Collected`

Observed feature emphasis:

- built-in light meter
- roll management
- per-frame exposure logging
- local/private storage messaging
- active release cadence with user-facing release notes

Implications for Exposure Logger:

- validates that there is real product demand for a dedicated film logging app
- confirms that local/private positioning is strong in this category
- shows that optional donations or support purchases can fit the category
- raises the bar on future feature scope if a built-in light meter is ever considered

Current likely differentiators for Exposure Logger:

- Android-first availability
- voice-assisted exposure entry
- optional GPS tagging
- full local backup/restore
- simple roll-centric workflow without cloud/account requirements

Positioning note:

Exposure Logger should lean into:

- fast logging in the field
- local-first/private data handling
- export/backup durability
- Android support

It should not try to position itself primarily against light-meter apps unless a metering feature is actually added.

## FilmLog

Reference:

- Reddit thread: <https://www.reddit.com/r/AnalogCommunity/comments/1qg3pfg/filmlog_a_free_film_archive_management_tool/>

Observed positioning:

- free film archive management tool
- oriented around archive sheets, contact sheets, directories, and collection statistics
- appears to focus on post-shoot archive organization rather than in-field exposure capture

Why it matters:

- this looks more complementary than directly competitive
- it supports the idea that the film-photo workflow can reasonably split into:
  - capture-time logging
  - archive-time management

Potential synergy with Exposure Logger:

- Exposure Logger can own shooting-time metadata capture in the field
- FilmLog can own archive-time organization after development and scanning
- a future export/import bridge could make the two tools complementary in one end-to-end workflow

Positioning note:

Exposure Logger should describe itself as a roll and exposure logging tool for use while shooting.

FilmLog-style archive tooling belongs later in the workflow and should not be blurred into the core promise unless a real archive-management feature is added.

Collaboration note:

If outreach ever happens, the best angle is:

- complementary workflow fit
- possible CSV/import interoperability
- shared interest in durable, local-first film metadata

The wrong angle would be pitching a fake integration before any concrete handoff format exists.
